About Me

I am a passionate evolutionary biologist dedicated to understanding the mechanisms of adaptation and evolution in extreme environments. My primary research organism is Astyanax mexicanus, a remarkable species that exists as both surface-dwelling and cave-adapted populations, providing a unique natural experiment in evolution.

Research

Current Projects

Convergent Evolution in Multiple Cave Populations of Astyanax mexicanus

Investigating the genetic basis of repeated trait loss across independently evolved cave populations. Using whole-genome sequencing and QTL mapping to identify whether the same genes underlie convergent phenotypes in different caves.

Funding: National Science Foundation DEB Grant #123456

Collaborators: Cave Biology Consortium, UNAM Mexico

Sensory System Compensation in Blind Cavefish

Examining how enhanced lateral line system and taste buds compensate for vision loss. Using behavioral assays, neuroanatomy, and transcriptomics to understand sensory system trade-offs.

Status: Manuscript in preparation for Current Biology

Metabolic Adaptations to Nutrient-Poor Cave Environments

Studying how cavefish have evolved altered metabolism, including increased fat storage and starvation resistance. Comparing metabolic rates and gene expression between surface and cave populations.

Funding: NIH R01 Grant (Co-Investigator)
